# Data Governance Policy

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Data Governance Policy is to establish the standards and procedures for managing data within the AgencyOS platform. This policy ensures compliance with global data protection regulations, specifically the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), by enforcing strict data segregation and retention principles.

## 2. Environment Data Segregation Policy

To ensure the security, privacy, and integrity of data within our multi-tenant architecture, the following data segregation principles apply:

### 2.1 Multi-Tenant Isolation
- **Logical Segregation:** All tenant data must be logically segregated at the database and application levels. Each record containing tenant-specific data must include a `tenant_id` (or equivalent identifier) to prevent cross-tenant data leakage.
- **Application Logic:** The application's data access layers and APIs must explicitly validate the context of the requesting user and restrict access exclusively to data associated with their respective `tenant_id`.
- **Encryption:** Data at rest and in transit must be encrypted. Tenant-specific encryption keys may be utilized where heightened isolation is required.

### 2.2 Handling of Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
- **Identification and Classification:** All PII must be identified and classified upon ingestion.
- **Minimization:** Only PII strictly necessary for the operation of the service shall be collected and processed.
- **Storage:** PII must be stored securely, utilizing field-level encryption where appropriate, and access must be restricted to authorized personnel and services on a strict need-to-know basis.
- **Processing:** AI models and agents must be configured to handle PII securely. Whenever possible, PII should be anonymized, pseudonymized, or redacted before being processed by LLMs or external services.

## 3. Log Retention and Purge Policy

In accordance with the GDPR storage limitation principle (Article 5(1)(e)), personal data must be kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which the personal data are processed.

### 3.1 Scope
This policy applies to all application logs, system logs, audit logs, and access logs generated by the AgencyOS platform that may contain personal data, user identifiers, or IP addresses.

### 3.2 Retention Period (30-Day TTL)
- **Standard Retention:** All applicable logs shall have a strict Time-To-Live (TTL) of 30 days from the time of creation.
- **Automated Purging:** Automated processes must be implemented to permanently delete or irreversibly anonymize log data immediately upon expiration of the 30-day TTL.

### 3.3 Exceptions
- **Legal or Regulatory Holds:** If log data is required for an ongoing legal investigation, security incident response, or regulatory compliance audit, a formal legal hold may be applied. Logs under a legal hold are exempt from the 30-day purge until the hold is officially lifted.
- **Aggregated/Anonymized Data:** Log data that has been fully anonymized and stripped of all identifiable information (e.g., for statistical analysis or performance monitoring) may be retained beyond 30 days.

## 4. Review and Enforcement
This policy will be reviewed annually or in response to significant changes in regulatory requirements or platform architecture. The engineering and security teams are responsible for implementing the technical controls necessary to enforce these policies.
